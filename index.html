I's... Introduction :)

I am a Computer Science major at Pomona college who is currently a junior. I love the problem solving aspect of computer science, and am hoping to use my technical skills for social causes. I love engaging in one to one interactions with people as I value learning from unique experiences of individuals. I am minoring in Psychology to honor my listener-empath character.

I currently serve as a Peer Advisor at the Career Development Office(CDO) of the Pomona College. I help my peers with their resumes and cover letters. Additionally, I plan and organize panels to connect alumni with current students. I prepare tiktok videos to advertise CDO's resources on our Instagram account. I connect to recruiters and help conduct events organized by the CDO. I present to my supervisors on monthly basis.

I also serve as the Captain of the Claremont Colleges Beach Volleyball Club Team. I manage a group of 20+ students by conducting twice weekly practices on Pomona's sand volleyball courts or indoor volleyball gym. I lead warm-up exercises, volleyball drills, and scrimmages. I report to the Athletics department, and have presented, and awarded a budget of $400 for the club.

I am the Vice-President of the Claremont Colleges Turkish Student Association. We are 14 Turks at the Claremont Colleges(9 in Pomona), and I help the president organize cultural events. I have presented the 2021 budget, and awarded $1400 for the club. Most recently, after the 7.8 Magnitude Turkish-Syrian Earthquake, I spearheaded a donation campaign which fundraised more than $16,000. We raised awareness by tabling outside the dining halls for an entire week, by pitching to professors and Deans of the college, and by interviewing to The Student Life Newspaper, and by interviewing to Pomona College Communications.

Lastly, I serve as the Sponsorship Lead of the 5CHackathon for which I connect recruiters from Big Tech Companies to Claremont Colleges Hackathon to request sponsorship and to strengthen ties. 5CHackathon used to be a big event for years until 2020 when Covid-19 Pandemic Hit and no one took a hold of the event.
